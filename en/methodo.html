Methodological approach

An OSINT investigation is never improvised.
Each step conditions the next, from defining the scope to the final report.
Click on each step to discover the details.

1

Scope definition

  • Identify who the target is and define the exact scope
  • List the authorised sources
  • Set the final objective of the mission
  • Avoids scope creep and ensures the relevance of collected data
2

Passive collection

  • Strict respect of OpSec: compartmentalised identity, isolated environment, anonymised queries
  • Sources exclusively open, no interaction with the target
  • Social media, public databases, search engines, dorks
  • Registries, metadata, archives
  • No trace left on the target
3

Cross-referencing & pivots

  • Cross data to identify connection points
  • An email address → a profile
  • A photo → a location
  • A username shared across multiple platforms
  • Each pivot opens new leads
4

Analysis

  • Structure, verify and prioritise information
  • Eliminate false positives
  • Assess the reliability of sources
  • Build a timeline or a mapping
  • Turn raw data into actionable intelligence
5

Reporting

  • Report clear, structured and actionable
  • Tailored to the recipient and their level of expertise
  • Every claim sourced, every conclusion justified
  • Enables informed decision-making without ambiguity
